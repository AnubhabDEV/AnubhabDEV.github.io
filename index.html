Knives Out

Directed by Rian Johnson

A terrific, authentic, modern-day Agatha Christie-style whodunnit...with an all-star cast.

At two hours and 10 minutes the film's on the long side, but it's an entertaining enough pastiche and we haven't had enough of these in the cinema lately.As a smartly crafted and shaped piece of entertainment, Johnson's movie comes off especially good in a moment when such mid-budget, story-driven movies have all but disappeared; it's a throwback that's also an outlier. The production design of the cluttered house in the film is fabulous.

Tenet

Directed by Christopher Nolan

A visually dazzling puzzle for film lovers to unlock, Tenet tries to serve up most of the cerebral spectacle audiences expect from a Christopher Nolan production. However, at times the plot overcomplicates itself and seems intentionally inaccessible and challenging.

Nomadland

Directed by Chloe Zhao

A woman embarks on a journey through the American West after losing everything during the recession.

Life on the road has never been so tenderly captured, politically alive and profoundly moving.Frances McDormand delivers a career-best turn in this gently meditative drama about the failure of the American dream.
